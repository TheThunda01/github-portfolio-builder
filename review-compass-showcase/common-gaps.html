Common gaps chart

Most review problems start as small planning gaps.

This page turns common methods problems into a student checklist. The goal is not to make the project perfect on the first try. The goal is to find weak spots early enough to fix them.

Fit
Review type is chosen before the purpose is clear.

Fix it by writing the purpose in one sentence and matching the review type to that purpose.

Scope
The question is too broad for the time, team, or evidence base.

Fix it by deciding what must be included, what can be excluded, and whether the project is better as a scoping review.

Search
The search cannot be reproduced.

Fix it by saving exact strategies, platforms, dates, limits, counts, and deduplication notes for every source.

Terms
Important synonyms, spelling variants, or controlled vocabulary are missing.

Fix it by expanding each concept and asking a librarian or experienced reviewer to peer review the search.

Limits
Date, language, or source limits are arbitrary.

Fix it by explaining the rationale and acknowledging what the limit could miss.

Screen
Screening criteria are vague or used by only one person without checks.

Fix it by piloting criteria, tracking conflicts, and using a second reviewer or verification sample when possible.

Extract
The extraction form is built after reading the included studies.

Fix it by drafting fields from the question and criteria, then piloting the form before full extraction.

Bias
Quality appraisal does not match the included study designs.

Fix it by selecting an appraisal approach that fits the evidence and writing reasons for judgments.

Synthesis
The team collects data without knowing how it will be summarized.

Fix it by naming the synthesis plan before extraction, including maps, themes, narrative groups, or effect estimates.

Report
The final paper does not follow the right reporting checklist.

Fix it by choosing PRISMA 2020, PRISMA-S, PRISMA-ScR, or a more specific reporting standard early.

Student moves

What to do before asking for mentor feedback.

Bring a short methods packet.

Include the question, review type, eligibility criteria, database list, draft search, screening plan, extraction fields, and the reporting checklist you plan to use.

Ask specific questions.

Instead of asking whether the project is good, ask whether the scope is feasible, whether the databases fit the field, and whether the extraction fields answer the review question.
