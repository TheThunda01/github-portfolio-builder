Why this exists

A practical bridge between a class idea and a review protocol.

Students often know what topic they care about, but the research process can still feel hidden. Review Compass makes the early decisions visible so a student can bring a better first draft to a mentor, librarian, or research team.

It slows down the first decision.

The site asks whether the student is estimating an effect, mapping a field, explaining experience, comparing reviews, or answering a time-sensitive question.

It keeps methods transparent.

Guidance is organized around explicit questions, eligibility criteria, reproducible searches, study selection, data collection, bias appraisal, synthesis, and reporting.

It encourages human review.

The app does not pretend to replace a mentor or librarian. It helps students prepare stronger questions and cleaner documentation before asking for expert feedback.

Methods route map

Choose the review type by purpose.

Cochrane guidance emphasizes that a well-formulated question shapes eligibility, searching, extraction, synthesis, and presentation. JBI guidance similarly recommends using the review purpose and PCC elements when planning scoping reviews.

1 Focused effect question

Use a systematic review or meta-analysis when the question can be framed with PICO.

2 Evidence landscape

Use a scoping review or evidence map when the goal is breadth, concepts, and gaps.

3 Experiences or meaning

Use qualitative or mixed-methods synthesis when findings need context and participant voice.

4 Urgent decision

Use a rapid review only when shortcuts are justified, named, and reported as limitations.

5 Mechanism and context

Use realist logic when the question asks what works, for whom, why, and under what conditions.

6 Existing reviews

Use an umbrella review when the unit of analysis is reviews rather than primary studies.

Cochrane-informed workflow

The planner follows the pieces students are expected to explain.

01
Define the question and scope.

State the objective clearly and decide whether the review is broad, narrow, or staged.

02
Pre-specify eligibility criteria.

Translate the question frame into inclusion, exclusion, study design, and grouping decisions.

03
Build a reproducible search.

Record databases, platforms, dates, full strategies, limits, deduplication, and counts.

04
Screen and extract consistently.

Pilot screening criteria and data forms before a team commits to the full workload.

05
Assess bias and synthesize responsibly.

Match appraisal and synthesis choices to the included study designs and the review question.

06
Report with the right checklist.

Use PRISMA 2020, PRISMA-S, PRISMA-ScR, or another suitable reporting standard.

Student-friendly by design

The tone is supportive because the work is already demanding.

Evidence synthesis requires careful decisions, but the first version of those decisions should not feel impossible. This site uses complete sentences, visible next steps, short definitions, and plain-language cautions so students can learn while they plan.

Built-in reminder

A generated plan is a draft. Students should still ask a librarian to review searches and ask a mentor to check the protocol, eligibility criteria, extraction fields, and synthesis plan.
